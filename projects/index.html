Canopy Height Estimation

Data Sciences for Farming Support Systems for Sustainable Crop Production Under Climate Change project (from Sept 2017) that involves multi-disciplinary consortium of research institutes under DST-JST scheme of Strategic International Collaborative Research Program (SICORP). The project involves data-science based approaches for high-end integration of information and agricultural sciences such as IoT, Bigdata analytics, crop modelling and omics (genomics and phenomics) to support high performance and sustainable agri-systems in semi-arid tropics of India.

  • Computer Vision, Digital Photogrammetry, Image Processing, Data Science
  • Structure from Motion, Stereo Correspondance, Depth-Map Estimation, Dense Point Clouds, Poisson Surface Reconstruction
  • Indian Agricultural Sector

Student Satellite Program

A 70 member student team dedicated to the vision of making IITB a centre of excellence in space technology. I am a part of the Electrical Subsystem of the Star Tracker-based Attitude Determination System.

  • Image Processing, Systems Engineering, CAD Designing, Hardware Descriptions, Data Structures
  • Feature Detection and Matching Algorithms, Range Searching Algorithms

Astronomy Animation Team

An initiative of the Astronomy Club at IIT Bombay to develop animation techniques through a scriptable, Python-based approach

Proposal for Optical Sensor for Tropical Rainforest Imaging

Proposed a space-borne image sensor to critically understand the effects of climate change and capture significant temporal remote sensing data after a critical survey and analysis of current satellite payloads to bridge the gap between the need for expensive airborne hyperspectral imaging while providing continuous data needed to map carbon stocks and flux at the broad spatial scales required throughout the year

Fast Adder-based Arithmetic-Logic Unit Implementation

Designed an Arithmetic-Logic Unit (ALU) which computes addition, subtraction, using Structural VHDL based on the fast adder Kogge-Stone Architecture to implement on a Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA)

Analysis of Data from Underlying Event Characteristics

Analysed 5.5 Gigabytes of data generated from over 2 million underlying event characteristics from charged particles in p − p collisions, using a proprietary particle physics data analysis tool called ROOT. Particles, which are observed as tracks in the detector and the leading track is the direction of the track along the particle with the highest momentum, were classified based on its rapidity for different multiplicity classes. The number of particles emitted in each event is given by the multiplicity class.

Motion-based Handwriting Recognition System
